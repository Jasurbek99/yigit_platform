Domestic Market Offset (Kwota ucin icerki bazara berlen)

To maintain quotas, some product is delivered to the domestic market. This is tracked daily per firm with supplier name (Geldi.M) and deducted from quota accounting. 16 firms tracked × daily entries = high-frequency data input.

Business rule: Quota is consumed by both export shipments AND domestic deliveries. The system must track both and show remaining quota in real-time. Negative balances exist (KWOTA HASABY shows negative values) — meaning some firms overdraw and need correction entries ("Duzetme").

8. The Complete Money Flow — Why Everything Works This Way

This section explains the REAL business logic behind the entire export operation. Understanding this is critical — every decision in the system (firm selection, quota tracking, shipment splitting) flows from this financial structure.

The Full Cycle

16 Firms have
BANK CREDITSMonthly payments due
→ need money →
EXPORT &
SELL abroadRevenue from sales
→ need permission →
Get QUOTA
from GovernmentExport license in KG
→ need to earn quota →
Sell to DOMESTIC
MARKET first1 ton domestic = 10 ton export
The Quota Formula: For every 1 tonne of tomatoes sold on the domestic (Turkmen) market, the firm earns the right to export 10 tonnes. This 1:10 ratio is set by the government and drives all planning decisions.

The $10,000 Rule — Why Firms Split Shipments

Sale > $10,000
MUST go to firm's bank account
Money deposited to bank account → used to repay credit → hard to withdraw for other operations
This is the official financial channel. Bank controls the money.
Sale < $10,000
Can be taken as CASH
Cash = flexibility = main operational money of the business
Used for transport costs, wages, daily operations, other business needs.

The Splitting Strategy

A single truck carries ~18 tonnes. At export prices, the total value often exceeds $10,000. To keep each sale under the threshold:

1 Truck
~18 tonnesTotal value ≈ $16,000
→ split into →
Firm A: ~9t
≈ $8,000< $10K → CASH ✓
 
Firm B: ~9t
≈ $8,000< $10K → CASH ✓
Documents per split truck
2 separate CMR documents
Each firm gets its own CMR waybill. The truck physically carries one load, but legally it's two consignments from two different export firms. Both CMRs processed by Sirin (Documents).
Quota impact
Split across both firms' quotas
If Firm A ships 9t and Firm B ships 9t, each firm's quota is reduced by their portion. This is tracked in ShipmentExportFirm (through-table). Example: "YGT-HMS" = Yigit H.J gets 9t quota deduction, Hemsaya gets 9t.
Who decides the split
Agamyrat / Gadam J (Logist)
They choose which 2 firms to use based on: remaining quota balance, credit payment schedule, cash flow needs. The platform shows a warning if any single firm's share exceeds $10,000 but does NOT auto-split.
60+ combinations explained
16 firms × 15 possible partners = many pairs
YGT-HMS, YGT-DM, HMS-MA, DM-GB, Tel Dow E+Tel Dow J, etc. Each combination appears in the Excel. Some firms pair more often based on quota availability and credit schedules.

Gapy Satyş — A Special Case

Gapy Satyş (Gate Sale) is NOT export. Another company comes to the greenhouse with their own truck and buys tomatoes directly at the gate. No border crossing, no customs, no CMR. This counts as domestic market sale, which means it contributes to the 1:10 quota ratio. Gapy Satyş is strategically used to build up quota for export.

Gapy Satyş characteristics
304 shipments this season (largest single "customer")
• Buyer's own truck (not YGT transport)
• No border, no customs, no CMR needed
• Sale at gate price (lower than export price)
• Counts toward domestic quota (enables 10× export quota)
• Cash payment at gate

Platform Automation Implications

⚠️ $10K Warning
When creating a shipment, if estimated sale value for any single firm exceeds $10,000 → show WARNING. Calculate based on: firm's share of weight × route's average sale price/kg. Do NOT block — just warn.
📊 Quota Balance Dashboard
For each firm show: domestic sold (kg) → earned quota (×10) → used quota (exported kg) → remaining. Warn when quota is running low. Factor Gapy Satyş into domestic totals.
💳 Credit Payment Tracker
Per firm: monthly credit payment due → total export revenue sent to bank → remaining credit balance. Shows whether the firm is on track to cover this month's payment. Data source: 1C (future integration, or manual entry for now).
📄 2 CMR Documents
When shipment has 2 export firms → system generates (or tracks) 2 separate CMR documents. Each CMR linked to its firm's share. Sirin sees both CMRs for the same truck in her documents queue. Deadline 13:00 applies to BOTH.
1C Бухгалтерия: Currently a separate system handling bank/accounting. Integration TBD — we're still studying the business processes. For now, credit payment info can be manually entered into the platform. Future: possible API or data sync with 1C for bank account balances and credit schedules.

Multi-Operation Platform Design

This app is not just for tomato export. The holding has factories, greenhouses, construction, logistics, and finance subsidiaries. The architecture uses a modular Django multi-app structure so future operations can be added as separate modules sharing the same core (users, vehicles, drivers, firms).

Now (Phase 1)
Tomato Export Module
Full shipment lifecycle, quota management, weekly planning, finance, reports — replacing this Excel entirely
Future Modules
Per subsidiary
Factory operations, greenhouse management, construction tracking, general logistics — each as a Django app sharing the core
Shared Core
Users, Roles, Vehicles, Drivers, Firms, Countries
One login, one vehicle registry, one driver list — used across all operations. Management sees reports across all modules.
Architecture principle: Logo Tiger = financial truth, YGT Platform = operational truth, Trip Mgmt = driver cost truth, Navixy = location truth. Each system owns its domain. The platform connects them without duplicating.

11. Pain Points & Automation Opportunities

Issues discovered from the data that the web application should solve.

1
90+ Missing Sales Reports
173 trucks sold but only 83 reports received. Arap's report delay blocks financial reconciliation. Solution: Automated reminders, mandatory report submission within X days of sale_end, dashboard visibility.
2
Quota Overdraw (Negative Balances)
KWOTA HASABY shows negative balances (e.g. Yigit: -2,603,000 KG in period 3). Firms use more quota than allocated, requiring correction entries. Solution: Real-time quota balance alerts, block shipment creation when quota < truck weight.
3
60+ Export Firm Combinations
Export firms are combined in complex ways (YGT-HMS, Tel Dow E+Tel Dow J, etc.) making quota tracking extremely complex in a spreadsheet. Solution: Multi-firm quota split per shipment with automatic deduction from each firm's allocation.
4
Marking Changes After Loading
"Markirowkasy uytgan bize habar berilmedi" (marking changed without notifying us). Last-minute marking changes cause document rework and delays. Solution: Change log with mandatory notification workflow when key fields change after loading.
